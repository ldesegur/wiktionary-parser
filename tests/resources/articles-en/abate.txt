===Etymology 1===
From {{etyl|enm|en}}, from {{etyl|fro|en}} {{term|abatre||to beat down|lang=fro}}, from {{etyl|LL.}} {{term|abbatto|lang=la}}, from {{term|ab-|lang=la||away}} + {{term|batto||lang=la}}, from {{etyl|la|en}} {{term|battuere||lang=la|to beat}}. [[cognate|Cognates]]: French ''[[abattre]]''

====Verb====
{{en-verb|abat|ing}}

# {{transitive}} To [[bring down]] or [[reduce]] to a lower state, number, degree or estimation.
#: ''Legacies are liable to be '''abated''' entirely or in proportion, upon a deficiency of assets.''
#* '''1597''', [[w:Francis Bacon|Francis Bacon]], ''[http://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/575 Essays or Counsels, Civil and Morall]'':
#*: Not that they feel it so, but only to '''abate''' the edge of envy.
#* '''1605''', [[w:William Shakespeare|William Shakespeare]], ''King Lear'', II.ii:
#*: She hath '''abated''' me of half my train
#* {{RQ:Authorized Version|Deuteronomy|34|7}}
#*: His eye was not dim, nor his natural force '''abated'''.
# {{intransitive}} To [[diminish]] in force or [[intensity]].
#: ''The pain '''abates'''.''
#: ''Order restrictions and prohibitions to '''abate''' an emergency situation.''
#* '''c. 1719''', [[w:Daniel Defoe|Daniel Defoe]], ''[[w:Robinson Crusoe|Robinson Crusoe]]'':
#*: {{...}} in the morning, the wind having '''abated''' overnight, the sea was calm, {{...}}
#* '''1855''', [[w:Thomas Babington Macaulay|Thomas Babington Macaulay]], ''History of England from the Accession of James II, Part 3'', [http://books.google.com/books?id=MN5CNdgbSTYC&amp;pg=PA267 page 267]:
#*: The fury of Glengarry{{...|, not being inflamed by any fresh provocation,}} rapidly '''abated'''.
# {{transitive}} To [[deduct]] or [[omit]].
#: ''We will '''abate''' this price from the total.''
#* '''1845''', [[w:Thomas Fuller|Thomas Fuller]], ''The Church History of Britain'', Volume 3, [http://books.google.com/books?id=OfefAAAAMAAJ&amp;pg=PA100 page 100]:
#*: Allowing nine thousand parishes ('''abating''' the odd hundreds) in England and Wales {{...}}
# {{transitive}} To [[bar]] or [[except]].
# {{transitive}} To bring someone down physically or mentally.
# {{transitive|legal}} To put an end to; to [[do away]] with.
#: ''to '''abate''' a nuisance''
#: ''to '''abate''' a writ''
# {{intransitive|legal}} To be [[defeat]]ed or come to [[naught]].
#: ''The writ has '''abated'''.''
# {{archaic}} To [[destroy]], or [[level]] to the ground
#* '''1542''', [[w:Edward Hall|Edward Hall]], ''The Union of the Noble and Illustre Famelies of Lancastre and York'':
#*: The kynge of Scottes planted his siege before the castell of Norham, and sore '''abated''' the walls.
